What is a game?

Raph Koster

Playing a game is the act of solving statistically varied challenge situations presented by an opponent who may or may not be algorithmic within a framework that is a defined systemic model.

Eric Zimmerman & Katie Salen

A game is a system in which players engage in an artificial conflict, defined by rules, that results in a quantifiable outcome.

Chris Crawford

  • Creative expression is art if made for its own beauty, and entertainment if made for money.
  • A piece of entertainment is a plaything if it is interactive. Movies and books are are non-interactive.
  • If no goals are associated with a plaything, it is a toy.
  • If a challenge has no "active agent against whom you compete," it is a puzzle; if there is one, it is a conflict.
  • Finally, if the player can only outperform the opponent, but not attack them to interfere with their performance, the conflict is a competition.
  • However, if attacks are allowed, then the conflict qualifies as a game.

The Skinner Box

Operant Conditioning Theory (Contexts)

Positive reinforcement (Reinforcement)

Occurs when a behavior (response) is followed by a stimulus that is appetitive or rewarding, increasing the frequency of that behavior.

Negative reinforcement (Escape)

Occurs when a behavior (response) is followed by the removal of an aversive stimulus, thereby increasing that behavior's frequency.

Positive punishment (Punishment)

Occurs when a behavior (response) is followed by a stimulus, such as introducing a shock or loud noise, resulting in a decrease in that behavior.

Negative punishment (Penalty)

Occurs when a behavior (response) is followed by the removal of a stimulus, such as taking away a child's toy following an undesired behavior, resulting in a decrease in that behavior.

Changing Behavior

  1. Give a clear path to the goal
  2. Monitor behavior
  3. Reinforce desired behavior with context
  4. Reduce Incentives for undesired behavior

Other Concepts

Avoidance Learning

A type of learning in which a certain behavior results in the cessation of an aversive stimulus. (Covering eyes from sunlight)

Extinction

Occurs when a behavior (response) that had previously been reinforced is no longer effective.

Token Economy

Token economy is an exchange system using the principles of operant conditioning where a token is given as a reward for a desired behaviour. Tokens may later be exchanged for a desired prize or rewards such as power, prestige, goods or services.

Chaining

An instructional procedure which involves reinforcing individual responses occurring in a sequence to form a complex behavior.

Introducing PeerQA

Key Concepts

  • Peer Review
  • A culture of open criticism
  • Open Discussion and Exploration
As an agent I am more likely to resepct the optinions of my peers than an outsider QA Analyst. Impower a culture of open criticism - When people know exactly what I say, social pressures encourage me to provide friendly, warm, and positive feedback. - My co-workers are my friends. I should give honest and accurate feedback. Agent Usage - I can see how I perform relative to my peers. - I can provide honest feedback to my peers. - I can see a clear path to improve myself. Notifications are critical to engagement Feedback should be monitored and moderated by the supervisor but not controlled. Agent's should feel liberated, not as if they are operating under a tightly controlled authoritarian environment. Supervisors will be given severely limited tools to engage in feedback to prevent abuse of authority. The tool is largely decentralized. It is open and self monitoring. The community is empowered not the management. Comments and feedback are public. Focus on The Socratic Method. - The Status Quo - My team is not experienced or skilled enough to give honest and accurate feedback. - The QA Analyst knows best. - Information is limited. Evaluations are only shared between agent, supervisor, and QA analyst. - Approach feels authoritarian and cold. - Agents may not feel engaged or take ONLY the QA seriously. - Prone to rigging/gaming the system. - Prone to human bias via the analyst or supervisor. - Only a single source of input. - Features - Advanced QA pairing engine to pair high performing agents with lower performing agents. Split down to granular levels. - A strong notification engine to keep agent's engaged into the system. - Tightly monitored communication channels that are public and moderated. - A quantative scoring model on calls. Scores range between 1 and 5 stars for each specific metrics. - A configurable metrics model. - A configurable forms model. - A configurable goals and rewards model. - Notifications - You have been assigned a new call to review - Someone has finished reviewing your call - Someone has started reviewing your call - Someone has commented on your call - A discussion is forming around a comments - Someone has upvoted/liked your comment - Someone has "boo"d your call. - Someone has replied to your comment - Someone on the team scored very highly. - Supervisor sent you a private message. - Recieved special mention from supervisor. - Somone on the team recieved special mention from supervisor. - A team member has achieved a goal, reward, or recoginition. - Someone has tagged your call with X. - You have recieved a new Badge. - You have gained in rank on the leaderboard. - You have lost in rank on the leaderboard. - Communication Channels - Supervisor <-> Agent is Public or Private depending on context - Agent <-> Agent is ONLY Public Supervisor Abilities - I can moderate discussion. - I can provie positive feedback to my team. - I can monitor metrics and configure rewards and goals. Goals / Reward Model - Each goal carries with it an assigned number of points. - These points are public and expire after 30 days(rolling). - A leaderboard ranks the top X employees + the current user. - All rewards and scores are "rolling" requiring continuous quality from agents. Punishment / Penalty Model - Punishments should be light but create negativate emotion. - Agents are given 3 "boo"s a week they can assign to calls. - A boo shows as a private message to another agent. - The message is one of many predefined statements. - Boo's are anonymous. Agent's do not know who sent them. - Punishment prevents boredom and makes reward more appealing. Badges Model - Inspired from LinkedIn's Skills Endorsements - Agents can tag other agents calls with predefinied tag types. - They can be either positive or negative. - Both positive and negative tags identify who assigned them. - After X amount of tags are recieved the agent recieves a corresponding badge to display proudly on their profile. - Badges and Tags are "rolling" requiring continuous quality from agents.
